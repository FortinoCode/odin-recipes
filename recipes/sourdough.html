What Even Is Sourdough?

Sourdough is a style of bread created by using a starter instead of store bought yeast. While more of a time commitment than a straight dough, sourdough makes up for it with a deep flavor profile.

Equipment

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Thermometer
  • Digital kitchen scale
  • Dutch oven
  • Large proofing bowl or bin
  • Banneton
  • A sharp blade for scoring, such as a lame
  • Wire cooling rack

Ingredients

  • 500g All-Purpose Flour
  • 390g Water (aim for around 90-95°F)
  • 60g Active starter
  • 10g Salt

Steps

Morning of Day 1

  1. Feed your starter

Evening of Day 1

  1. Pour 390g warm water into a large mixing bowl
  2. Add 500g of all purpose flour to the water
  3. Mix by hand until just combined and let rest for 30 minutes
  4. Add 60g of active starter and 10g of salt to the mixture
  5. Mix by hand until fully incorporated
  6. Use your fingers to break apart any large clumps of flour
  7. Transfer to a separate bowl or proofing bin and let rest for 30 minutes
  8. Perform a series of five stretch and folds
  9. Repeat steps seven and eight three more times
  10. Cover your dough and let proof over night (8-12 hours)

Morning of Day 2

  1. Check your dough to see if it has doubled in size. Don't worry if it hasn't, just let it proof for a bit longer
  2. Once dough has doubled in size, transfer to a lightly floured counter top
  3. Shape your dough and place in a floured banneton
  4. Allow your shaped dough to proof for another two to four hours (if you are unsure if your dough has finished proofing you may perform the poke test)
  5. While your dough is proofing, place your dutch oven into your oven and preheat to at least 500°F for a minimum of one hour
  6. Gently transfer the dough from the banneton to a floured surface and score it
  7. Carefully transfer the scored dough to your dutch oven, cover it, and place into your oven
  8. Reduce heat to 475°F and bake covered for 20 minutes
  9. Remove the lid from the dutch oven and continue to bake for another 25 minutes or until the loaf is a deep brown
  10. Remove your finished bread from the dutch oven and place on a wire rack to cool for at least a half hour
