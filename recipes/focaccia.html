What Even Is Focaccia?

Focaccia is a classic leavened Italian bread perfect for sandwiches or as a side! This recipe will use rosemary, but focaccia is great for topping experimentation!

Equipment

  • Large mixing bowl
  • Thermometer
  • Digital kitchen scale
  • Large proofing bowl or bin
  • Wire cooling rack
  • Cast iron pan
  • Proofing bag

Ingredients

  • 500g All Purpose flour
  • 400g Water
  • 10g Salt
  • 75g Olive oil
  • Additional olive oil for topping
  • Three sprigs of fresh rosemary
  • Additional salt for topping

Steps

  1. Add flour and warm water (aim for roughly 90-95°F) to your mixing bowl and mix until just combined
  2. Let rest for 30 minutes
  3. Add olive oil and salt and mix thoroughly (your dough will be sticky and wet, this is good!)
  4. Transfer dough to your proofing bin and let rest for 30 minutes
  5. Perform a series of five stretch and folds
  6. Repeat steps 4 and 5 for the next two hours (five total stretch and folds). You should notice your dough is becoming stronger after each series of folds!
  7. Allow dough to double in size
  8. Transfer to a well oiled cast iron
  9. Using your hands gently spread your dough until it fills the pan
  10. Place cast iron into a proofing bag
  11. Preheat your oven to 475°F and finely chop your rosemary leaves
  12. After the dough has once again doubled in size drizzle your dough with olive oil, give it a light dusting of salt, and sprinkle rosemary across it
  13. Finally, dimple your dough and place into the oven
  14. Bake for around 30 minutes or until dough is a golden brown
  15. Carefully remove from your cast iron pan and transfer to a cooling rack
  16. Let sit for at least thirty minutes and enjoy!
